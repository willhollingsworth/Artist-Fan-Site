Main Content

Moderat consists of Sascha Ring, Gernot Bronsert and Sebastian Szary.

The three musicians' work together represents their most accessible material, fusing pop songwriting with elements of progressive house, IDM, and garage. The group earned acclaim for their live performances, and became increasingly popular with the release of their first three albums, which appeared between 2009 and 2016. Following the release of Live, their first concert recording, Moderat went on hiatus in 2017, then returned in 2022 with fourth studio album More D4ta.

The trio released the 12" Auf Kosten der Gesundheit (consisting of four tracks and a series of locked grooves) on Ellen Allien's BPitch Control, but they placed Moderat on a shelf for several years. They put together a stylistically varied and often frantic self-titled album, featuring guest vocals from Paul St. Hilaire and Eased, and released it on BPitch in April 2009. Worldwide tours and festival appearances occurred under the Moderat name for the next four years, but the group didn't return to the studio until 2013 when the album II was released by Mute Records in North America and appeared elsewhere on Monkeytown. The album sold briskly for an electronic release, reaching number ten on the German charts and respectively peaking at 11 and 23 in Switzerland and Austria. Two EPs, Last Time and Bad Kingdom Remixes, landed in 2014 before the group returned to the album format two years later with the aptly titled III. Live, recorded at a sold-out concert that took place in Berlin on June 5, 2016, appeared in November. In 2017, the group announced they were taking an extended hiatus. This lasted for three years before the isolation of the COVID-19 pandemic lockdowns prompted them to once again start working together remotely. The fruits of their labors were released in 2022 as the album More D4ta (an anagram of "Moderat 4"), which had a woozy, creepy, dystopian vibe.
